Oct 23, 1942

Arrived safely

Omaha has a very big station and has escalators. I slept thru most of Iowa but what I saw of it was just like Nebraska except the towns seem to have industries too. Chicago as I wrote before is a big noisy dirty (with smoke) city. At Chicago any reservation on the Trail Blazer wasn't there so I took the Admiral one hour later. I didn't see much of Philadelphia because the train reached North Philadelphia over an hour late so I had to rush to catch the Swarthmore train.

I reached Swarthmore about 9 AM. I met Dean Hunt, went to my room (about 12x20), then I went back to arrange my courses. They are:

- Mechanics of Materials
- Fluid Mechanics
- Principles of Electrical Engineering
- Elementary Economics

Swarthmore is a very good school. Kinda exclusive too in fact their library is bigger than Sacts' City Free (130,000 volumes). Swimming pool, indoor track, and tennis courts outdoor too. I'll try to get a catalog and mail you one.

I'm tired now so I'm going to sleep. Signing off at 7:20 PM.

George
