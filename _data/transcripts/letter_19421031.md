Oct. 31, 1942

Hi!

Well after one week here I still can't tell you too much about Swarthmore College, less about Swarthmore town, and practically nothing about Philadelphia. Finally got my baggage here on Wed., (arrived last Fri.) so now I'm starting to catch up. Finally. About my financial status - out of the $600 I'm to work $360 for Goord leaving $300 a year. I haven't even written to the Bank of America yet - I haven't found a bank in this town yet, neither a post office. Anyway, I think I'll just have them mail me what I need the first semester soon as they give me a bill. I still got $10.64 with me which should last some time. Anyway I guess I'll sorta hibernate here till I graduate (?). Another thing - they have 3 semester a year so if everything goes well, I should be out by the first of 1944. But heck! I don't know where I'll get the money for next year. Oh well let it go! Yesterday I got a notice saying I had to take Phys. Ed. 2 hrs a week ( The Army requires it of juniors and seniors) so I'll have to go out for that. Also it seems that there's a swimming test you have to pass every semester (they have a pool here) which was supposed to be taken before Nov. 1. My job - well I just wash cups or wipe for about 2.5 hours every night. I'm supposed to put in 3 hours - not hard work. Food is pretty good ($350 a year) but you have to know how to eat fast. At first I used to go away half filled - maybe I was self-conscious about my table manners - but not anymore. I used to wonder and still do, as a matter of fact, how those big fellers could get along on so little. One thing that bothered me was that you have to break a slice of breach (no rice) in then butter it and then eat it 1/2 at a time. I need to find myself cutting the whole bread and then remember to break it into two. Also, I'm learning to cut meat with the right hand. I don't think Willie has a chance of coming to Swarthmore nor do I think it advisable because the school as a rule doesn't admit students above the sophomore year and there doesn't seem to be any biochem course as such here; they just have advanced organic chem. You can get an A.B in chem here. The engineering course here isn't extensive as at Cal. Everyone takes the same course thru the junior year, and in the fewer year we divide into M.E., C.E., and E.E. and that's all, or airplane design or dream (specialization). However, I think that as long as you don't try to get into graduate work the courses are just as good as Cal.

Here's the tentative program:

1. Strength of materials, fluid mechanics, principles of electric engineering, and elementary economics
2. Thermodynamics, electrical engineering, structural theory and design, and economics
3. Internal combustion engines, refrigeration and air conditioning, engineering economy, and CE (continued from last sem)
4. Heat power, thesis, and 2 electives

Ask W what he thinks about it. I sent a change of address to Popular furnace so don't wonder if it doesn't come. Well how about Tule Lake has about sending me some Tulcan Dispatchers or telling me something about it. I guess Meyer's going to school again. Bye now(?)

O yeah! I wrote to Lafayette Radio Co. I asked for the money. I also ordered some parts to fix the set I got here. They have a collection of classical records here which is supposed to be one of the largest in the country (over 3000) which you can go and play anytime you feel like. Also last Mon. night we had a movie (free) here a French version of "Algiers," and a Walt Disney comical festival. They also have a school broadcasting station over which they play records from this allocation, and present various other programs.

Well I trust you are all well, Sam. I'll do my best out here and hope Willie can get out too.

George
