Hi! Everything okay? Well, we lost the Haverford game - 14.13 pretty close tho. This is the first time they have beat us in about ten years and they were unbeaten and untied this year. Mrs. Tanaka and Walter Kato came over, and I showed them over the campus and talked things over with them until 5:30, when I had to go eat because I work as soon as I eat. We also had a homecoming Sat. so there were quite a few grads around.

This afternoon I attended a SCRR Swarthmore committee on Race Relations meeting, at which James Farmer Inter-Racial secretary of the Fellowship of Reconciliation spoke. He is a niger and spoke on the various techniques they use to combat racial discrimination. He advocated non-violence methods such as boycotts, sit-down strikes and other legal methods. Most of the methods however could not be applicable to Nisei because we are a very small minority. Frankly I don't see any solution to the problem except by universal practical application of democratic and Christian ideals which isn't the case today.

George
