Hi! Well, today marks my twentieth year on this mass of matter which we call earth. By some miracle I'm still ticking away and even more miraculous is the fact that I'm here.

Tonite I actually caught up with the cutting Collection - finally. Right after finishing washing glasses in the kitchen (I washed tonite) at 8:10 I went over to Bond + Heard Bach's Mass in D min. It lasted until 10 (about 30 some odd sides), but it was really good. I never knew that an full orchestra and voices could mix + give such a wonderful effect. The voices were used just like any other instrument as the violins or flute.

That was in the large room on the main floor. The records are upstairs in a small room about 12x12 - about 3,000 of them! Well I went up + listened until about 12:30 + just got back. Boy! they really have everything Strauss, Haydn, Bach, Beethoven, Wagner - anybody practically. I'll have to go over often.

Today, it was clear for a change but its pretty cold. I hope we have snow soon.

We had classes yesterday (Thanksgiving) even. I thought that it was going be be a holiday so I didn't prepare my homework so I did it in class during the problem session

At Collection yesterday we had a musical program by a piano, English Horn + violin trio (pretty good except [horn]) + by the choir. They had about 50-75 (male + female) in it + it was really good. That's one thing you don't have in tulelake is a big good choir. I think Tule lake has better soloists though. One of the songs they sang was "Listen to the Lambs." Remember that one, Maiju? Was used to sing it in the glee clubs at Lincoln with Mrs North.

There are quite a number of articles in various magazines about the evacuations. I looked them up in the Reader's Guide. I think I'll read up on those + send you summaries on them + my opinions about them so be waiting.

The Tulean Dispatches don't seem to be coming. How about it?

Well, its getting kinda late (1:30) so I'll have to close now. I usually don't get to bed till about 12 so its not too late. Some fellows are still walking around outside + somebody's typing + a radio is still going somewhere. This should reach you about next Saturday - have a good Sunday.

George

The three hundred came from Sac'to so I have 300. — 401.10 — of which all but 3.05 is in the bank.

About - those Christmas cards - I ordered 50 for a dollar. They print your name on them for you Is it proper to send them out, or do you think I ought to throw them away?

I'm going to see Dean Hunt tomorrow about the arrangements for Christmas vacation. It starts on the 17th + is about 2 weeks. I think I'll be able to stay here at school + work at Phillie or something. I'm sure that I can get some sort of job.

George Inoue
