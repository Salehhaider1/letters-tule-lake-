Thurs. Dec. 10, 1942

Hi! Well, we did the reading over S.N. The girl read "The murder of Lidice" by Edna St. Vincent Millay; it appears in the Oct. 19 issue of "Life". Well, look it up if you still have it; incidentally its the issue that came just after I left Tule Lake. This time there was no director in the control booth like last night so I was rather shaky at first. I guess we were both rather nervous. Oh well, practice makes perfect so I'll get better as time goes on I hope. Today in collection a former Austrian who worked with the League of Nations + also former international secretary of the World Student Service fund spoke on the topic of "War and Education." He discussed the education of the German youth, how the Germans closed all schools in Poland, Czechoslovakia, Yugoslavia, and a few other conquered countries. He stressed the need for education in the post war reconstruction. He also mentioned en nisei + how our education is vital for the post-war period + went on to suggest that we should be an important factor in reestablishing good feeling between Japan + U.S. This statement orta disturbed me cause I have my doubts as to our ability to cope with such an immense, tremendous problem; at least not the condition we're in now. George
