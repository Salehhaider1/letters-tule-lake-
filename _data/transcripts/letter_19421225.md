Hi!

Merry Christmas + a Happy New Year! I guess this letter won't reach you until after New Years Day. How was Christmas? It was very different from those we've observed in the past wasn't it?

Well, I've been working in the shop right along about from 9-5 every day so I've been fairly busy. Also the last two nights I've been invited out to dinner - on Wed. Dave Linton + his girl + I went to the Bradlys' - Mr. Bradley is an engineer for Philco Corp + Mrs. Bradley is speech instructor here at Swarthmore - I met her when I made my voice recording. She's had experience on stage directing movies + television. Last night I went to Dave's home + we had a roast duck. Dave's mother is a Swarthmore graduate + had a MA from Columbia, + his dad is a prof. of anthropology at Columbia. Mr. Linton wasn't home however. Mrs. Linton told us about her experiences with the American Friends' Service in France after the first world war.

Today I'm invited over to the Wilcoxen with the Hunts. So, you see, I'm fairly busy.

I guess you've seen the article about the meat shortage in next weeks (dec. 28) Life. Has it affected you yet? Well, I haven't as yet. although Mrs. Linton was telling me that people who had ordered turkeys were only getting halves of them because a trainload of them were wrecked. Also people were getting a quarter lb. of butter for the Christmas dinner - + of course - coffee - everybody saves the left overs + the grease"; also the Hunts use sacchini instead of sugar in their coffee.

Oh yeah, here are the list of things I got for Christmas: Mr. + Mrs. Bradley gave me a couple of Irish Linen handkerchiefs. Dave Linton + Harriet Bender (girl friend of Daves) gave me a Mc gregor scarf made in Scotland. Then the Hunts gave me a very loud shirt - red, blue, yellow, black, etc - which seems to be in fashion out here, a can of shoe polish, a pair of socks, + candy. Also the Wilcoxes gave me a couple of handkerchiefs too. So you can see that I made quite a haul. Really, everybody is so good. I can't hardly understand why. I intended to get something for the Hunts at least but I didn't get around to it. I guess I'll have to get something tomorrow. Why doesn't Willie or Miyo or somebody write them + thank them for taking care of me? Oh yeah, I bought some toys for the Oyama children which I haven't sent yet. Its kinda late, but -. I bought a little metal car, a small toy bugle (I got a sneaking suspicion that we got the same thing last year - red white + blue plastic ~~~ - how about it Miyo?) and a sets of jacks + a rubber ball; also some of the candy I got + a christmas card.

I didn't get anything for you - if you really want something, well, write me + I'll see what I can do. I'm still fairly rich.

I didn't send a card to Dr. Day cause I didn't have his address. Solly. Them cards I sent - I guess they were sorta hap-hazard - miss quite a few? Well, I wrote until I ran out + then I quit.

remember that sorta greenish pants? Well, I took it to the village to be dyed or navy blue - haven't gotten it back yet, but I guess I'll have one more to wear. That one I bought just before I left is too wide around the belly so it'll have to wait till I grow up or something.

I got Mrs. Oyama's address thru Mr. Olani I wrote to him in English with some mikuyo but he answered in English, so next time, I'll use all English. At least I think it was he who wrote it because of all the grammatical errors, but the writing was very good - somewhat like Tomokogua.

Also, I've finished another book - "Contempt of Freedom" by Polanyi - a series of essays examining + criticising the Soviet socialistic state. His main point is that in a "planned" economy, everything cannot go "as planned, and planning takes away the initiative. He makes a comparison of our supposedly "supervised," + their "planned" society. He also gives various statistics + facts showing that their system is worse than ours. I guess I'll have to read a book giving the other side of the argument too - I've read two books against socialism + none for as yet.

Well so long for now

George.
