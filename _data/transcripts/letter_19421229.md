Hi! Guess you've heard the good news already - about Willie being able to come here to Swarthmore. Deldn Hunt told me yesterday that he had received word from the Nat. Stud. Rel. Council in S.F. that you would come. Yippie!! I guess I'll be seeing you towards the end of January. Remember those advices I wrote about travelling - try to put the things you don't need right away in freight when you get to Reno - above 300 lbs that is - also check the itinerary to see that you have enough time there to arrange to send by freight because it might take some time. Might as well bring most of the clothes you have - nothing you can't use though. Books too - don't bring any you don't think you'll have any use for - there's a good library here. Oh yeah - bring along all the food you can - especially oranges + apples + don't count on buying food on the train but jump off the train at stops + grab something then. The train from Chicago to Philke doesn't supply free water cups (it does at 1¢ each).

If you're meeting Sumio in Chicago, better tell him which station to meet him at (La salle or Union) because he told me that when Dave Okada came thru, he missed him because he was waiting at the other station. Also check up on reservations if you have any when you buy that ticket in Reno. I wouldn't wear too good of a clothes because you might not get a chance to change - specially because they might be crowded even in the rest rooms (see those pictures in Life?) (well, it wasn't that bad when I went thru.) Well, there's my advices for the present, honey chik - more later if + when they occur in my little brain; also I guess there's time enough if you all wants to ask any questions. Also, how about all those papers + such in willies name - you gonna transfer them to pop or what?

Received your letter, christmas card + check today - thanks. I guess I'll wait till Willie comes to buy any clothes.

Say, that thing about those kid stealing those cltr is bad. Who were they + what did they do about them? I think somebody was crazy in the first place to allow such things into the camp- the crazy fools

I sure hope that it isn't a sign that we're "cracking" - that the confinement is changing our morals, our ideas of what is right + what is wrong. Heaven knows that it isn't all our fault, but it is an acid test of us all. This is the first time I've felt like crying since I came out here. May God help us to be strong. I remember one collection speaker telling us how Hitler + the Nazis are starving the people in Poland, but selling liquor at a cheap price + have closed all their schools - that was to change the whole Polish race into a bunch of illiterate drunkards, to make them into an inferior race because they weren't German. I'm wondering if the internment won't have the same general effect upon us - at least in that general direction, if not to the same degree - hope not.

That thing, or should I say "affaire" at Manzanar also ain't so hot is it? I read about it in the New York Times Daily newspaper but didn't mention it to you because the authorities might have suppressed the news + you might not have known about it. The article wasn't on the front page, however - thank Heavens. Also, the Poston "incident" had a writeup in Newsweek. That or should I say those aren't such hot writeups - heck - they wouldn't write anything good about them would they? Darn 'em. Blast 'em. Hope the Tule lake thing doesn't get any publicity.

Well, I'm doing fine - having lots to do. So don't worry about this end of the line, but I will worry about you all - until the end of the war + then some - can't help it.

Heard that Mr. Shimono quit the mess too. Is he going out to the farm too? I think Mr. Jokanai can last long - with the sugar, coffee, meat, can goods, etc rationed, he should think that he couldn't take it; well, hope not, somebody's gotta cook.

Solong George
