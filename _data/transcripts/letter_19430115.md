Hi!

Will, today is father's birthday. Happy birthday!, and may you have to celebrate many more! For these many years you have taken care of us. I cannot begin to repay you - not even was I to become rich as a Rockefeller, I still could not do enough. For the Japanese spirit of oyako-ko, as it was extended to us so often by Mr. Saki-yawn, tells us that you can never be repaid in dollars and cents, nor can a certain number of years. I shall certainly adhere to those principles to the best of my ability, for all that I am is because of you and mom.

You certainly haven't had an easy life. The bits of your pest, as you sometimes used to tell us about then - schooling, railroad work, town labor, etc. - sure doesn't sound soft. And then, when you thought you had finally achieved some sort of security, every day is blasted away, or rather, at first sight it does seem that way, is it so? Oh no! You have raised and nurtured these children - their lives which no amount of money or scientific effort could create. No only that, but you have also given us a good stout in life - an education such as you never had the opportunity to acquire. Even now, you are giving up a major portion of our family treasury to give me, and soon Willie too, a college education. A couple hundred years ago, only kings, princes, and other royalty could get an education; now, even I have this privilege. It is up to us three to capitalize on your efforts and become worthy of you.

God has certainly taken good care of you and us, and now, more than ever before, we realize how much we need His guidance, care, and boundless love. Let us be thankful and pray for continued blessings.

Your son,

George
