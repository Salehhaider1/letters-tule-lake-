Wed.

By the time you receive this letter Miysho will have left for Philadelphia to join me. I know that both of you will miss her a great deal especially because she is the baby of the family. We will do our best to take good care of her and base our decisions as you would. Of course, we'll see her when she arrives on soon and try to make all arrangements for her to work for about 2 weeks before school starts. One of the first things we want to do after our finals are over is to go to eat chop suey.

Miyo's letter that came today said you would go to see Mrs. Toshima if permission were granted - I hope that they were all in good health.

I think that it was a good decision on your part to let her come out on a work leave as that her train fare would be taken care of - not to mention the 50 grant which she will receive in Phili.

We will probably bring Miyohs to her on Sunday and sleep at the college - whether Japanese girl said she would make arrangements as she would sleep here on Sunday night. Then on Monday Miyohs can go to see Mrs. Patterson about the job. Both George and I will finish our finals on Thursday, Oct 21 and will be able to see Miyohs more often. I phoned Mrs. Patterson's office yesterday and was told that they would take care of all arrangements such as job and place to stay.

I hope you have sent the wire telling us which train rate she is to take, station to arrive (there are 3 stations in Phili), and time of arrival - in her letter she said about 11 AM. I expected to receive the wire today but it didn't come - maybe tomorrow.

Anyhow - don't worry about Miyahs - even though you may not realize it, she has grown up considerably in the last year during and after evacuation. The next time you see her you will be amazed to see even greater changes due to maturity. Furthermore George and I will do our best to take care of her.

William

I was very glad to see pop's letter in his own handwriting, even though Miyah and mom's letters do convey your thoughts, I too am looking forward to the day when we can all be together again - and hope that we children can live up to your expectations. I also wish that you will seriously begin to consider relocation because I don't think it is right for any human being to live under such conditions if it is at all possible to get away. Of course there are many many arguments pro and con and many "ifs" to consider of a decision is difficult to make. In the meantime please take care of yourselves and don't worry about us.

George

We had chow mein the other night. Only, the "noodles" were about the size of a chopstick and cooked until they looked and tasted like skinny French Fried potatoes. However there was shoyu and rice to go with it. It's over a year since I've eaten real Japanese food
